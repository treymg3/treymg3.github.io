## About My Project
The SCHOLAR project seeks to explore how the integration of data-driven technologies and Community Health Worker programs can improve health outcomes in under-resourced communities.

My research topic for DREAM this summer will be a continuation of this work in alignment with Karam et al. (2023), Hernandez et al. (2024), and Veeramreddy et al. (2025) which studied the use of Community Health Worker data and Social Determinants of Health for predicting emergency department readmissions. The research scope involves using and improving an existing machine learning model to assist Community Health Workers and help prevent hospital readmissions. The existing modeling workflow includes methods such as Random Forest modeling for feature importance and explainability. This is important because Random Forest models can help identify which variables are contributing most to readmission prediction while still providing some level of interpretability. Since this project involves healthcare data, the goal is not only to improve prediction, but also to understand which variables may be meaningful for Community Health Workers, clinicians, researchers, and healthcare organizations.

The early stage of the project focuses on understanding the existing SCHOLAR data pipeline, becoming familiar with the structure of a Kansas population dataset, and using that dataset to practice data science techniques before applying the workflow to Sinai Urban Health Institute data.

My DREAM objectives are:

1. Reproduce prior work with new data from Sinai Urban Health Institute (research arm of Sinai Chicago).
2. Better understand the existing data pipeline and methodology for data analysis.
3. Contribute to machine learning model reproducibility by carefully documenting data preparation, modeling steps, and evaluation decisions.
4. Contribute to integrating Electronic Medical Records data to the current pipeline and modeling.
5. Explore possible future research directions involving patient satisfaction, sentiment, Community Health Worker notes, and readmission prediction.
