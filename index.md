## About Me
My name is Milton Garrett III and welcome to my site! After completing my first year in DePaul University’s Master’s in Computer Science program (graduating in late 2027/early 2028), I am thrilled to have been selected for the Distributed Research Apprenticeships for Master’s (DREAM) program. DREAM is designed to provide research experience and mentorship to master’s students who are transitioning into computing from non-computer science backgrounds. As someone whose academic and professional background includes biochemistry, health informatics, clinical research, and healthcare quality improvement, I am incredibly grateful for the opportunity to continue developing as a computer science researcher.

Through DREAM, I have joined the lab of Dr. Roselyne Tchoua, where my work connects data science, machine learning, Social Determinants of Health, Community Health Worker programs, and hospital readmission prediction. My personal research interests lie at the intersection of computing systems, data reliability, healthcare data infrastructure, and applied machine learning. I am especially interested in how scalable computational methods and reproducible data pipelines can improve decision-making in high-impact domains such as healthcare and finance.

Outside of my studies and research, I enjoy watching and following sports, watching courtroom television shows, listening to high-quality orchestral music, and appreciating the music and artistry of Michael Jackson.

If you have any questions or would like to collaborate, please feel free to reach out using through email or add me on LinkedIn.

## About My Advisor

My DREAM research advisor is Dr. Roselyne Tchoua, an Associate Professor in the School of Computing at DePaul University. Her research focuses on data analysis, machine learning, natural language processing, information extraction, and interdisciplinary applications of data science.

Dr. Tchoua’s work is especially meaningful to my project because she studies how computational methods can make complex data more useful in applied domains such as healthcare. ## About My Project
The SCHOLAR project seeks to explore how the integration of data-driven technologies and Community Health Worker programs can improve health outcomes in under-resourced communities.

My research topic for DREAM this summer will be a continuation of this work in alignment with Karam et al. (2023), which studied the use of Community Health Worker data and Social Determinants of Health for predicting emergency department readmissions. The research scope involves using and improving an existing machine learning model to assist Community Health Workers and help prevent hospital readmissions.

The early part of my work involves getting acclimated with the data pipeline, the existing research methodology, and the full data analysis workflow. I am currently training with data obtained from a Kansas population to understand the model structure, data-cleaning process, feature preparation steps, and evaluation methods. The broader intent is to apply this understanding to the SCHOLAR project using Sinai Chicago data.

My DREAM objectives are:

1. Reproduce prior work using newly obtained Social Determinants of Health data from Sinai Chicago, thereby contributing to reproducibility of the current research methods.
2. Clean, pre-process, and integrate newly obtained Electronic Health Record data to enrich future machine learning models.
3. Better understand the existing data pipeline and methodology for data analysis.
4. Contribute to machine learning model reproducibility by carefully documenting data preparation, modeling steps, and evaluation decisions.
5. Explore future research directions involving patient satisfaction, sentiment, Community Health Worker notes, and readmission prediction 

Reference:
Karam, K., MacCabe, K., & Tchoua, R. (2023). Leveraging Community Health Workers and Social Determinants of Health for Predicting Emergency Department Readmissions. Proceedings of the 2023 Fifth International Conference on Transdisciplinary AI.
