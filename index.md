## About Me

My name is Milton Garrett III. I am currently pursuing a Master’s in Computer Science at DePaul University and will graduate in late 2027 /early 2028. My academic background includes biochemistry, health and medical informatics, and clinical research. Because of that background, I am especially interested in projects that connect computer science, healthcare data, machine learning, and real-world patient outcomes.

My research interests lie at the intersection of computing systems, data reliability, and complex real-world infrastructures such as healthcare and finance. Through my prior work in health informatics and multi-site quality improvement research, I observed how limitations in data extraction, reporting accuracy, and system interoperability can significantly impact downstream outcomes. These experiences motivated my transition into computer science, where I am developing foundations in data structures, algorithmic reasoning, and systems thinking.

I am particularly interested in exploring how scalable computational methods and robust system design can improve data integrity and decision-making in high-impact domains. Ultimately, I aim to contribute research that bridges applied systems challenges with rigorous computational inquiry.

## About My Project
The SCHOLAR project seeks to explore how the integration of data-driven technologies and Community Health Worker programs can improve health outcomes in under-resourced communities.

My research topic for DREAM this summer will be a continuation of this work in alignment with Karam et al. (2023), which studied the use of Community Health Worker data and Social Determinants of Health for predicting emergency department readmissions. The research scope involves using and improving an existing machine learning model to assist Community Health Workers and help prevent hospital readmissions.

The early part of my work involves getting acclimated with the data pipeline, the existing research methodology, and the full data analysis workflow. I am currently training with data obtained from a Kansas population to understand the model structure, data-cleaning process, feature preparation steps, and evaluation methods. The broader intent is to apply this understanding to the SCHOLAR project using Sinai Chicago data.

My DREAM objectives are:

1. Reproduce prior work using newly obtained Social Determinants of Health data from Sinai Chicago, thereby contributing to reproducibility of the current research methods.
2. Clean, pre-process, and integrate newly obtained Electronic Health Record data to enrich future machine learning models.
3. Better understand the existing data pipeline and methodology for data analysis.
4. Contribute to machine learning model reproducibility by carefully documenting data preparation, modeling steps, and evaluation decisions.
5. Explore future research directions involving patient satisfaction, sentiment, Community Health Worker notes, and readmission prediction 

Reference:
Karam, K., MacCabe, K., & Tchoua, R. (2023). Leveraging Community Health Workers and Social Determinants of Health for Predicting Emergency Department Readmissions. Proceedings of the 2023 Fifth International Conference on Transdisciplinary AI.
